A lot to hold right now

It takes real courage to reach out when things feel this much.

You shared that you are living with the death of someone you love. Right now it sounds like the feelings are close to the surface and hard to hold alone. That is an understandable response to loss — not a weakness.

You mentioned that rest and sleep, waves of sadness, and feeling alone are weighing on you most. These are some of the most common ways grief shows up in the body and mind, and you are far from alone in feeling them.

You also said you do not feel very supported right now. Having even one steady, understanding person to talk to can make a real difference — and that is exactly what a free conversation is for.

Grief coaching is not therapy or medical treatment. It is a calm, supportive space to be heard, to make sense of what you feel, and to find small, gentle steps forward — entirely at your own pace.

A gentle invitation

If it feels right, you are warmly invited to a free, no-obligation 30-minute conversation. There is nothing to prepare and nothing to prove — just a chance to talk and see if it helps.
